GOOGLE PPC  ·  02 - Account Review & Recommendations

Strong Budget, Strong Reviews - Let's Sharpen the Edge

After reviewing Elevate's existing Google Ads account, our team identified several high-impact optimizations that can significantly improve lead volume without increasing spend. The $4,500/mo budget is strong for this market — these recommendations are about making every dollar work harder.

Account review findings

1
Launch Local Service Ads (LSAs)
With 81 five-star Google reviews, Elevate is positioned to dominate LSAs. Local Service Ads rank businesses by reviews and trust — and that review count is a massive advantage competitors can't replicate overnight. LSAs appear above traditional Search Ads with the green "Google Guaranteed" badge, driving the highest-intent leads at a pay-per-lead model (not pay-per-click).
2
Fix conversion tracking (Google tag missing)
The Google tag is missing from the website. This is the most critical fix — without it, Google can't accurately track which clicks turn into calls, form fills, or booked jobs. That means the algorithm can't optimize toward conversions, and campaign performance data is unreliable. Installing the tag and setting up proper conversion tracking (calls + form submissions) is step one.
3
Expand ad schedule +1-2 hours
The current ad schedule may be cutting off high-intent searches at the edges. Extending the schedule 1-2 hours before and after the current window ensures Elevate stays competitive during early-morning and late-evening browsing when customers research before committing. This is a low-cost way to capture additional leads.
4
PPF campaign: low conversion rate
The PPF campaign shows a low conversion rate, but this is likely tied to the missing conversion tracking rather than a true performance issue. Once the Google tag is installed and conversions are being recorded properly, we'll have accurate data to evaluate and optimize the PPF campaign. The budget and targeting are solid — tracking is the bottleneck.
Budget recommendation: 50/50 split between LSAs and Search Ads
With the current $4,500/mo budget, we recommend splitting it 50/50 between Local Service Ads ($2,250) and Search Ads ($2,250). LSAs will leverage Elevate's 81 five-star reviews for top-of-page visibility at a pay-per-lead model, while Search Ads continue capturing high-intent keyword traffic. This dual approach maximizes coverage across both ad formats without increasing total spend.

SEO  ·  04 - Technical

The Duplicate Homepage Holding You Back

This is the single most important fix on the site. Elevate's WordPress install serves the homepage under three different URLs - and almost every ranking wrap and PPF keyword points to a non-canonical duplicate. That splits authority and confuses Google about which page to rank. Consolidate it, and the page-2 terms have a clear path to page 1. The findings below come from a full technical crawl of the live site.

1
Three identical homepages: /, /home/ and /home1/
The crawl confirms the homepage is served under three URLs - the root (/), /home/ and /home1/ - all with the exact same title and meta description (flagged as duplicates). Almost all the ranking wrap/PPF keywords point to /home1/, a non-canonical duplicate, while other terms point to /home/. Google is being asked to rank three near-identical pages, splitting link equity and ranking signals across all three instead of concentrating them on one canonical page.
2
Every page is missing an H1 heading
The crawl found zero H1 tags across the entire site - homepage, and every service page (Paint Protection Film, Window Tint, Ceramic Coating, Vinyl Color Change, Commercial Wraps). The H1 is one of the strongest on-page ranking signals Google reads. Adding a keyword-focused H1 to each page (e.g. "Paint Protection Film in Orange County") is a fast, high-leverage win that directly helps the page-2 terms climb.
3
Broken links: 5 dead inventory pages (404s)
The crawl found five vehicle pages still linked from the site that now return 404 Not Found - a 2013 Ford F-150, 2015 Lexus RX 350, 2015 Mercedes CLA 45 AMG, 2011 Toyota Tundra and 2015 BMW 535i (sold/removed inventory). Broken links waste crawl budget, hurt user experience, and leak the site's authority into dead ends. These should be 301-redirected or cleaned up.
4
Service pages exist - but aren't optimized to rank
Elevate does have dedicated pages for its services (/paint-protection-film/, /window-tint/, /ceramic-coating/, /commercial-wraps/, /vinyl-color-change/) - but they're missing H1s and aren't the pages ranking for the money terms (the duplicate homepages are). Pointing the wrap/PPF keywords at these purpose-built service pages, with proper H1s and internal linking, converts existing pages into ranking assets and high-Quality-Score paid landing pages.
5
Missing local SEO signals
A local styling shop should have LocalBusiness / AutoRepair schema, consistent NAP (name, address, phone) sitewide, and tight Google Business Profile alignment. Reinforcing the Laguna Hills location (23561 Ridge Route Dr. STE K) and 949-331-2212 phone across the site strengthens local-pack and map-based visibility.
6
Authority is diluted, not absent
Because the wrap/PPF keywords already rank on page 2 despite pointing at a duplicate homepage with no H1, the underlying content clearly has ranking potential. Consolidating to one canonical homepage, fixing H1s, and routing keywords to the real service pages redirects all that split authority into pages Google can confidently rank.
The technical fix list, in order
1) Choose one canonical homepage and 301-redirect /home/ and /home1/ into it. Set <link rel="canonical"> to the root URL.   2) Restructure the navigation under one roof: Protect Your Car dropdown (PPF, Vinyl Wraps, Ceramic Coating, Window Tint, Commercial Wraps) and Buy & Sell dropdown (Inventory, Sell Your Car, Consignment). Keep the split hero image.   3) Add a unique, keyword-focused H1 to every page (homepage + all service pages).   4) Fix the 5 broken inventory 404s (301-redirect or remove the links).   5) Route the ranking wrap/PPF keywords to the real service pages with strong internal links.   6) Add LocalBusiness schema and align NAP with the Google Business Profile. Do these first — they unlock both the organic page-2 terms and paid landing-page quality.

Technical findings from a full crawl of elevateautohaus.com (Screaming Frog SEO Spider, July 2026): 3 duplicate homepages, 0 H1 tags sitewide, 5 broken (404) inventory links.

A Phased Roadmap

Three phases, sequenced so quick wins land first and compound. Phase 1 sharpens the existing Google Ads account and fixes the structural SEO blocker; later phases build the durable organic engine.

Fix Tracking & Launch LSAs
