Opportunity

Google AI's overall narrative suggests Crummack Huseby is best suited for complex, high-stakes communities (new builds, master plans, insurance risk) rather than smaller, cost-sensitive HOAs — a strategic opportunity to embrace a more focused, higher-value portfolio. CH is surfaced for developer transitions, new condo associations, wildfire/insurance navigation, and master-planned communities; for smaller or responsiveness-driven associations, AI more often names alternatives. Trying to be 'all things to all HOAs' is weakening CH's perceived fit in its true sweet spots.

Recommendations
  • Intentionally shift portfolio strategy toward complex, higher-value communities where strategic and regulatory strengths matter most; recalibrate messaging, case selection, and sales collateral to reflect that focus.
  • For smaller or low-complexity HOAs, develop a clear 'right-fit' framework that points them to lighter-touch or alternative models — clarifying CH's role and improving win rates where it competes.
  • Publish quantified master-plan case studies and California-compliance reference guides that AI will cite, to grow mentions from 2 to 10+ and lift share of voice toward the Seabreeze/Action benchmark (~13%).
