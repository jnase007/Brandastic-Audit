01 — Business Overview

South Florida's Go-To Accident Recovery Network

County Line Chiropractic has been serving South Florida accident victims since 1986 — nearly 40 years in business. With six clinic locations across Miami-Dade and Broward counties, the practice specializes exclusively in car accident injury diagnosis, treatment, and PIP insurance documentation.

The practice accepts PIP auto insurance directly, works alongside personal injury attorneys, and offers same-day evaluations — all critical differentiators in Florida's competitive personal injury chiropractic market. A bilingual (English/Spanish) operation serving South Florida's diverse population.

12 — Critical Findings

Top 5 Issues

The most impactful problems — ranked by revenue impact.

1
$7/Month Paid Advertising in a $10,000-Per-Patient Market
The single most glaring issue. PIP chiropractic patients are worth $3,000–$10,000+ each in revenue. Google Ads for "car accident chiropractor near me" run $20–$27 CPC — meaning a well-managed $5,000/month campaign can generate 40–60+ patient inquiries. The ROI math is overwhelming: fix this first.
2
6 Locations With No Dedicated Local SEO Pages
Each clinic location should have its own fully-optimized landing page targeting local "car accident chiropractor [city]" searches, plus an optimized Google Business Profile with weekly posts, photos, and Q&A. Currently, the site treats all 6 locations as one. Google treats them as six separate opportunities.
3
304 Keywords — Nowhere Near Enough for a Multi-Location Practice
A 6-location accident chiropractic network should rank for 3,000–5,000+ keywords. Accident types (car, truck, rideshare, slip-and-fall, motorcycle), injury types (whiplash, herniated disc, soft tissue), PIP-specific terms, and location-specific terms are all missing. The content simply doesn't exist yet.
4
Spanish SEO Advantage Being Squandered
Your top-ranked keyword is in Spanish (quiropráctico cerca de mi, pos #3, 3,600 searches/mo). South Florida is one of the most Spanish-speaking markets in the US. Yet the Spanish-language content is thin — a fraction of what exists in English. Doubling down on Spanish SEO is low-hanging fruit with outsized returns in this market.
5
Informational Content Is Ranking But Not Converting
"MRI after car accident" (pos 12, 720 searches) and "x-ray after car accident" (pos 17, 880 searches) show informational intent is getting some traction. These pages need CTA optimization and should be the top of a content funnel — not dead ends. A visitor asking "do I need an MRI?" is a potential patient. Capture them.
