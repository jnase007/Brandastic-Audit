01 — Executive Overview

A Cleanup & Recovery Plan

Viking Bags is not starting from zero. The brand has meaningful authority, a large organic footprint, and existing visibility across both traditional search and AI-generated search environments. The problem is the trendline: the latest Semrush snapshot shows the site moving in the wrong direction. This audit should be read as a cleanup and recovery plan — stabilize the SEO foundation first, then build toward AI-driven ecommerce discovery.

Real authority, declining visibility

Viking Bags carries an Authority Score of 49 and a large organic keyword footprint, so the domain has not lost its ability to compete. But prior SEO efforts appear to have left behind over-optimization, crawl inefficiencies, and missed ecommerce content opportunities. The priority is to remove signals that look over-optimized or low-quality, improve crawlability, and strengthen the product and collection pages that drive revenue.

The plain business takeaway

Semrush reports a Site Health score of 84%, an AI Search Health score of 77%, 1,000 crawled pages, 376 blocked pages, 606 pages with issues, and only 10 healthy pages. Viking Bags also has measurable AI visibility — an AI Visibility score of 26/100, 2.22K mentions, 7.42K cited pages, and a monthly AI audience of 22.57M. Fix the SEO foundation first, then use schema, installation content, ecommerce landing pages and ChatGPT ads to bridge search visibility to product revenue.

That creates a real foundation for future AI search and ChatGPT ads strategy — but only after the core SEO cleanup is addressed.

Already cited for motorcycle topics

The Viking Bags blog is already being cited by AI systems for motorcycle-related topics — motorcycle clubs, beginner bikes, motorcycle types, engine issues, model comparisons and motorcycle culture. That informational authority is a real asset.

The opportunity: connect content to revenue

The blog earns AI visibility, but that authority needs to flow toward revenue-driving product and collection pages. Installation content, schema and ecommerce landing pages can bridge that gap — and prepare the brand for ChatGPT ads.

Platform-by-platform footprint

04 — High Points

What's Already Working

Viking Bags has several strengths that make recovery and growth realistic. The issue is decline and cleanup, not a lack of foundation.

Existing brand authority

The site has an Authority Score of 49 and still maintains a sizable organic keyword footprint. The domain has not lost its ability to compete — the issue is that visibility is declining and the current SEO foundation needs cleanup, not that the site has no authority.

Strong ecommerce footprint

The second Screaming Frog crawl identified 636 indexable URLs — product pages, collection pages, blog posts and core landing pages. Product and collection pages are present, crawlable in part, and have the potential to become stronger revenue drivers.

Metadata exists across most pages

The site does not have a widespread missing-metadata problem. Title tags and meta descriptions are generally present. The issue is quality, length and over-optimization rather than basic implementation — a much easier fix.

Canonicals appear mostly clean

The crawl did not reveal widespread canonical problems among accessible indexable URLs — a positive ecommerce signal, since Shopify sites often struggle with duplicate URL paths, collection/product variants and parameter handling.

Blog content is an AI visibility asset

05 — Core Pain Points

Where the Recovery Work Lives

Six core pain points are holding the site back. Most stem from prior SEO efforts that left behind over-optimization and crawl inefficiencies — each is fixable as part of a structured cleanup.

1
Over-optimized title tags from past SEO efforts
Semrush flagged 219 pages with too much text in the title tags; Screaming Frog showed hundreds of long titles across product, collection and landing-page templates. Long, keyword-heavy titles make the site look over-optimized, reduce readability and lower CTR. The goal is to rewrite titles around buyer intent, fitment, product type and trust — not just shorten them.
2
Installation PDFs are a missed AI & SEO opportunity
Valuable installation content lives inside PDF manuals on the Shopify CDN — proprietary how-to content answering exactly what customers and AI ask (fit, tools, install time, difficulty, modifications, compatibility). Converting these PDFs into HTML guide pages under an Installation Resource Hub turns existing assets into crawlable, linkable, citable how-to content.
3
AI search blocking needs validation
37.6% of crawled pages are blocked (376 total), with 375 blocked from AI/search bots. But most appear to be low-value Shopify paths — cart, checkout, account, internal search, filtered collections, app/system URLs — that shouldn't be indexed. The priority is not to unblock everything, but to confirm the right pages (clean product URLs, collections, blog, guides, installation pages) are accessible.
4
Crawl throttling & 429 responses limit auditability
Both Screaming Frog crawls showed persistent 429 Too Many Requests responses — 865 URLs still returned 429 on the stronger second crawl. This suggests the site is throttled or blocked at scale during crawling. If Googlebot, AdsBot or AI crawlers hit the same friction, visibility could be lost across product, category, blog and paid landing pages. A Phase 1 technical issue.
5
Internal linking isn't supporting revenue pages
The crawl showed 158 URLs with 2 or fewer unique inlinks and 435 URLs with 5 or fewer. Viking Bags has blog content that already earns AI visibility, but that authority needs to flow toward product and collection pages. Internal linking should connect high-performing informational pages to commercial destinations — one of the clearest ways to turn visibility into revenue.
6
Product, collection & template-level issues need cleanup
34 pages with multiple H1s, 4 missing H1s, 7 missing meta descriptions, 492 long titles and 456 long meta descriptions (Screaming Frog), 605 low text-to-HTML pages, 605 blocked-resource warnings and 17 duplicate H1/title pages (Semrush). Not all equally urgent, but together they show a site that needs structured cleanup — prioritizing product, collection and bike-specific landing pages.

07 — Prioritized Recovery Plan

The Recovery Roadmap

Four phases, sequenced so each builds on the last — stabilize the SEO foundation first, then strengthen revenue pages, then build AI-ready content and ChatGPT ads readiness.

Phase 1 — Foundation
Priority: High
Stabilize the Technical Foundation
What to fix
  • • Diagnose & resolve the 429 crawl throttling — confirm Googlebot/AdsBot/AI bots aren't blocked
  • • Validate robots.txt & blocked pages — keep clean product, collection, blog & installation URLs accessible
  • • Confirm the right Shopify low-value paths stay blocked (cart, checkout, account, internal search, filters)
  • • Audit sitemap inclusion vs indexable priority URLs
  • • Prioritize crawl paths to revenue-driving product & collection pages
  • • Confirm tracking for organic, paid, AI-assisted & ecommerce paths
Business impact

Crawl access is the foundation for everything else. If crawlers are being throttled, Viking Bags risks losing visibility across product, category, blog and paid landing pages. Confirming clean access and the right blocking rules protects existing rankings and ensures search engines and AI systems can read the pages that drive revenue.

Phase 2 — Optimization
Priority: High
Clean Up & Strengthen Revenue Pages
What to fix
  • • Rewrite over-optimized titles around buyer intent, fitment, product type & trust (219+ flagged)
  • • Trim long meta descriptions; add the 7 missing ones on priority pages
  • • Fix the 34 multiple-H1 pages & add the 4 missing H1s
  • • Improve thin / low text-to-HTML pages, prioritizing product & collection templates
  • • Strengthen internal links from blog content to product & collection pages
  • • Lift inlink support for the 158 URLs with ≤2 inlinks and 435 with ≤5
  • • Focus on product pages, collection pages & bike-specific landing pages first
Business impact

Removing over-optimized signals and strengthening revenue pages helps existing rankings recover and improves how AI interprets the catalog. Cleaner titles lift CTR, better internal linking pushes authority toward commercial pages, and stronger collection/product copy improves both organic and paid landing-page quality.

Phase 3 — Structured Data & AI
Priority: High
Installation Hub, Schema & AI Visibility
What to build
  • • Convert installation PDFs into HTML guide pages under an Installation Resource Hub
  • • Structure each guide around fit, tools, install time, difficulty & compatibility questions
  • • Add HowTo & FAQ schema to installation & support pages
  • • Validate Product & Offer schema across product pages
  • • Add Breadcrumb schema; confirm Organization & Website schema
  • • Add Article / BlogPosting schema to motorcycle blog content
  • • Improve Open Graph & product image metadata
  • • Link installation guides to the matching product & collection pages
Business impact

Installation guides answer exactly the questions buyers and AI ask before and after purchase. Turning proprietary PDFs into crawlable, structured HTML — backed by schema — creates authoritative how-to content that supports traditional SEO, AI visibility, product confidence and future ChatGPT ads readiness, all from assets Viking Bags already owns.

Phase 4 — Authority & Market Share
Priority: High / Ongoing
Ecommerce Discovery & ChatGPT Ads Readiness
What to build
  • • Bike-specific fitment & buyer-guide landing pages
  • • Collection-level content connecting blog authority to products
  • • "Will this fit my motorcycle?" fitment & compatibility content
  • • Saddlebag / luggage comparison & selection guides
  • • Installation difficulty, tools & accessory-compatibility content
  • • Strengthen the motorcycle blog as an AI-citation engine that links to products
  • • High-intent ecommerce landing pages for paid & AI traffic
  • • Prepare product & landing pages for ChatGPT ads
Business impact

Once the foundation is clean and content is AI-ready, the opportunity is to bridge search visibility to product-driven revenue. Connecting the blog's existing AI authority to product and collection pages — and standing up high-intent ecommerce landing pages — grows organic and AI-driven discovery, increases citations, and prepares Viking Bags to convert that visibility through ChatGPT ads and paid media.

Next Steps

Viking Bags has real authority, a large organic footprint and measurable AI visibility — it just needs a cleanup. Stabilize the foundation, strengthen revenue pages, turn installation PDFs into AI-ready content, and Viking Bags can reverse the decline and grow ecommerce discovery through AI and ChatGPT ads.
