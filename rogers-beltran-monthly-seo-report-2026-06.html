Perception drivers & opportunities

Medium timeframe

Among Spanish-speaking and Latino clients, Rogers | Beltran has an emerging perception of trust and respect, but this is underdeveloped relative to the opportunity indicated by AI queries about undocumented and immigrant victims.

You show up in the 'Bilingual & Culturally Competent Representation' and 'Spanish-speaking/Latino trust' drivers, but with only one or two mentions, while AI answers about immigrant and undocumented victims mostly highlight other firms and educational resources. Given your Southern California base and bilingual capability, there is a much larger emotional and cultural territory to claim here.

Recommendations
  • Deliberately position Rogers | Beltran as a safe, culturally fluent option for Latino and Spanish-speaking clients facing catastrophic injury or criminal exposure.
  • This means more Spanish-first explainer content, visible Latino attorney/staff voices in media and reviews, and explicit reassurance (in both languages) on issues AI flags as key fears — immigration status, police contact, and fee transparency.
  • Aim for your bilingual strength to become a primary, not secondary, driver in how AI describes you.
Short timeframe

Rogers | Beltran LLP is perceived as a boutique, client-centered trial firm that resists 'mill' behavior, which strongly differentiates it from high-volume competitors in AI narratives.

Perception drivers show unanimous reinforcement (5/5) that the firm offers personalized attention, direct access to attorneys, and a non-mill approach. AI summaries also describe Rogers | Beltran as the opposite of a quick-settlement shop, emphasizing professionalism and attentive service. In a landscape dominated by mass-marketing firms, that boutique and relationship-driven identity is a clear brand advantage.

Recommendations
  • Elevate 'boutique trial team for people who hate billboard firms' to a central brand story across all touchpoints — website copy, intake scripts, bio lines, and media.
  • Train your team to consistently articulate how your low-volume, high-attention model translates into better strategy and emotional support, and encourage AI-visible reviews to emphasize that contrast explicitly.
  • This will position you as the de-facto choice for skeptical, high-anxiety clients AI describes in its own answers.
Short timeframe

AI Mode sees Rogers | Beltran as highly ethical and transparent on fees and expectations, but price-sensitive clients still worry they could find cheaper representation, diluting perceived value.

You are associated with 'Contingency-Based Access to Justice' and 'Transparent Fees & Cost Education,' and AI commentary emphasizes clear explanations and fair arrangements. Yet negative drivers show fee-sensitive prospects fear they might get similar representation at lower contingency or flat fees elsewhere, especially when comparing you to volume firms that advertise aggressively on affordability.

Recommendations
  • Lean into radical transparency about value rather than competing on being 'cheap.' Standardize clear, plain-English fee explanations (including typical ranges of case costs, examples of net recovery after fees, and how you handle cost risk) and share them via FAQs, consult scripts, and third-party interviews.
  • Where ethically appropriate, highlight case examples where clients paid a standard contingency but ended up significantly better off versus initial lowball offers or prior counsel's approach, so AI and humans alike see you as high-value, not high-priced.
Opportunity

Rogers | Beltran sits at 0% share of voice with zero mentions and zero citations in Google AI Mode — invisible in broad AI answers. The category itself is barely visible (leader Wilshire Law Firm holds just 0.5%, with 99.1% in the long tail), so this is an open, winnable lane. The firm's business model — high-touch, low-volume, serious cases only — positions it to maximize impact and net recovery on catastrophic and life-altering matters, but only if it consciously declines lower-value, misaligned work and communicates that boundary clearly in AI-facing channels. AI's drivers associate Rogers | Beltran with high-stakes, complex cases and contingency access, but flag tension around boutique-level fees for 'routine' matters. When Google AI advises cost-sensitive or lower-stakes users, it points them to mega-firms or mid-market providers, not boutiques — so the firm must make its 'serious case' identity explicit and machine-readable.

Recommendations
  • Explicitly reframe Rogers | Beltran as a 'serious case' firm across Google Business, legal directories, and website copy — spelling out the case types the firm is built for (catastrophic injury, wrongful death, felony-level and rights-implicating criminal cases) and routing smaller matters to a vetted referral network.
  • Make the Latino/Spanish-speaking segment a deliberate growth pillar: build Spanish-first journeys (practice pages, FAQs, bilingual bios, Spanish directory listings, Spanish-language media) so AI aligns the brand with bilingual trial expertise.
  • Attach the firm to high-credibility, AI-cited platforms (Best Law Firms, Benchmark Litigation, bar-association rankings, trial-lawyer and criminal-defense association leadership) and publish verdict/results data — the sources AI leans on (bestlawfirms.com, counselhound.com, forthepeople.com) to build the recommendation set.
