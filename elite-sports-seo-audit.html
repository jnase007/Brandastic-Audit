01 — Executive Overview

A Cleanup & Ecommerce-Growth Plan

Elite Sports is not starting from zero. The brand has a meaningful search footprint, blog authority, product and category coverage, and existing citations across major AI platforms. The problem is the trendline: the current Semrush snapshot shows pressure across organic visibility and authority signals. This audit should be read as a cleanup and ecommerce-growth plan — stabilize the SEO foundation first, then turn general martial-arts authority into product recommendations for high-intent ecommerce prompts.

Real footprint, downward pressure

Elite Sports carries an Authority Score of 39 and a large organic keyword footprint (19.2K), so the domain still competes across combat sports. But organic traffic and keywords are declining, backlinks are down sharply, and the SEO foundation needs cleanup before AI search and ChatGPT ads can become a scalable growth channel. The priority is technical cleanup, then connecting existing content authority to product and category pages that drive revenue.

The plain business takeaway

Semrush reports a Site Health score of 78%, an AI Search Health score of 81%, and 1,000 crawled pages — 954 with issues, only 8 healthy, 35 redirects, 3 blocked. Elite Sports also has measurable AI visibility — an AI Visibility score of 30/100, 410 mentions, 2.09K citations, 775 cited pages, and a monthly AI audience of 3.67M. Fix the SEO foundation first, then use schema, FAQs, buyer guides, ecommerce landing pages and ChatGPT ads to bridge search visibility to product revenue.

03 — AI Search Visibility

AI Platform Visibility

Elite Sports is already visible across the major AI platforms, with an overall AI Visibility score of 30/100, 410 mentions, 2.09K citations, 775 cited pages and a monthly AI audience of 3.67M. The issue is not whether Elite Sports is visible — it's whether that visibility supports product discovery and revenue.

Already cited for combat-sports topics

Elite Sports content is already cited by AI systems for BJJ, martial arts, boxing, UFC, rash guards, gis, training content and general combat-sports questions. AI Overview holds the largest cited-page share at 57.4%. That informational authority is a real asset.

The opportunity: connect content to revenue

Current visibility skews informational. That authority needs to flow toward revenue-driving product and category pages. FAQs, schema, buyer guides and ecommerce landing pages can bridge that gap — and prepare the brand for ChatGPT ads.

Platform-by-platform footprint

Semrush reports platform mentions of AI Mode 119, ChatGPT 108, AI Overview 97 and Gemini 86, with AI Overview carrying the largest cited-page share at 57.4%. The footprint is broad and balanced — a strong starting position. The work is to deepen owned, crawlable, structured content so AI systems cite Elite Sports product and category pages directly, not just blog posts.

05 — Core Pain Points

Where the Growth Work Lives

Six core callouts are holding the site back. Most stem from an informational-first footprint and template-level SEO issues — each is fixable as part of a structured cleanup that connects existing authority to ecommerce intent.

1
AI visibility needs to become more ecommerce-focused
Elite Sports already appears across AI platforms (AI Mode 119, ChatGPT 108, AI Overview 97, Gemini 86), but visibility skews toward BJJ, boxing, UFC and general training questions rather than product discovery. The issue isn't whether Elite Sports is visible — it's connecting that visibility to product and category pages so AI recommends products, not just cites articles.
2
High-intent AI topic gaps are clear
AI topic-gap data shows major misses where competitors are mentioned and Elite Sports isn't — Boxing Equipment & Gear (41.36K AI volume, 55% commercial), MMA/UFC Gloves (12.1K, 51%), BJJ/MMA Spats (3.41K, 51%), MMA Gloves (2.03K, 73%), MMA Clothing (1.82K, 46%). These have strong commercial intent and should map to category pages, product pages, buyer guides, FAQs and schema.
3
Technical SEO health needs cleanup
Site Health 78%, AI Search Health 81%, 1,000 crawled pages — but 954 have issues, only 8 are healthy, 35 are redirects, 3 blocked. Top Semrush issues: 950 low text-to-HTML pages, 375 duplicate H1/title tags, 206 titles with too much text, 10 duplicate titles, 7 duplicate-content issues. The site needs template cleanup, metadata refinement and stronger page-level structure.
4
Crawl data shows metadata, depth & indexing issues
Screaming Frog found 2,537 URLs (1,839 indexable, 698 non-indexable): 233 redirects, 160 x 429s, 31 x 503s, 8 x 404s, 1 x 500. Also 493 long titles, 1,132 long meta descriptions, 98 missing meta descriptions, 69 duplicate titles, 739 indexable URLs deeper than depth 3, and 553 with 5 or fewer inlinks. The blog is large and many articles sit deep in the crawl path.
5
Blog content needs AI-ready formatting
The content-optimization export flagged 57 pages, mostly for paragraphs that are too long. That's a direct AI-readiness issue — long, dense sections are harder to extract into AI answers, snippets and structured responses. Elite Sports' cited blog base is strong; it just needs shorter paragraphs, cleaner heading hierarchy and summary blocks to become more citable.
6
Schema, FAQs & hubs should bridge SEO and AI search
Elite Sports should use structured content to connect ecommerce pages with AI behavior — Product, Offer, Review/AggregateRating, Breadcrumb, FAQ, Article/BlogPosting and collection schema, plus size guides, care guides, buyer guides, comparisons and "best for" recommendations. The goal: make pages easier for both search engines and AI to understand, cite and recommend.

07 — Prioritized Plan of Action

The SEO + AI Roadmap

Four phases, sequenced so each builds on the last — stabilize the technical foundation first, then strengthen product & category pages, then build AI-ready content and schema, then expand topic coverage and ChatGPT ads readiness.

Phase 1 — Foundation
Priority: High
SEO Cleanup & Technical Foundation
What to fix
  • • Resolve duplicate H1/title patterns (375) and duplicate title issues (10)
  • • Rewrite overlong title tags (493) and meta descriptions (1,132); add 98 missing ones
  • • Validate the 7 duplicate-content errors
  • • Investigate 429, 503, 404 & 500 responses
  • • Clean up parameter crawl waste (especially srsltid URLs) & redirect chains
  • • Validate XML sitemap coverage & confirm key product/category pages are indexable
  • • Fix crawl-depth issues for important pages (739 deeper than depth 3)
Business impact

This stabilizes the SEO foundation before adding new AEO work. It improves crawl efficiency, organic visibility, click-through rate, product discovery and paid landing-page quality — so both search engines and AI systems can cleanly read the pages that drive revenue.

Phase 2 — Product & Category
Priority: High
Product & Category Optimization
What to fix
  • • Prioritize categories tied to AI & organic opportunity: boxing equipment & gloves, MMA gloves & clothing
  • • BJJ gis, rash guards, spats/compression pants; martial-arts apparel, training equipment & gear bags
  • • Women's MMA gear and kids' martial-arts gear
  • • Each priority category: clean title, unique H1, buyer-focused intro copy & FAQ section
  • • Validate Product schema; add internal links to related categories & guides
  • • Add clear sizing, care & use-case content to product and category pages
Business impact

These are the pages most likely to convert organic and AI-assisted shoppers. Optimizing them improves category rankings, product discovery, conversion potential and AI ecommerce relevance — turning general martial-arts authority into product recommendations for high-intent prompts.

Phase 3 — AI Readiness & Schema
Priority: Medium-High
AI Readiness, Content Structure & Schema
What to build
  • • Reformat the 57 flagged pages: shorter paragraphs, cleaner heading hierarchy, top-of-page summary blocks
  • • Add FAQ sections, comparison tables, "best for" recommendations & pros/cons blocks
  • • Validate Product & Offer schema (price & availability) across product pages
  • • Add Review / AggregateRating schema where legitimate
  • • Add Breadcrumb, Organization, Website & FAQ schema
  • • Improve BlogPosting/Article schema; add HowTo schema for care/training/sizing content
  • • Link blog content and guides to matching product & category pages
Business impact

AI systems rely on clear, structured content — long paragraphs and weak heading hierarchy make answers hard to extract. Cleaner structure plus schema improves the chances of being cited in AI answers, ranking in AI Overviews, and appearing for ecommerce recommendation prompts, while supporting rich results in traditional search.

Phase 4 — Topic Expansion & ChatGPT Ads
Priority: Medium / Ongoing
Topic Expansion & ChatGPT Ads Readiness
What to build
  • • Best boxing equipment for beginners; best MMA gloves for training
  • • MMA gloves vs boxing gloves; best BJJ spats & compression pants
  • • What to wear for MMA training; best at-home martial-arts training equipment
  • • BJJ gi sizing guide; boxing glove size guide; how to choose a rash guard
  • • Women's MMA gear guide; kids' martial-arts gear guide; BJJ gi care & washing guide
  • • Strengthen the blog as an AI-citation engine that links to products
  • • Build topic clusters around BJJ, MMA, boxing, Muay Thai & martial-arts gear
  • • Prepare conversion-ready product & landing pages for ChatGPT ads
Business impact

These topics map directly to AI search behavior and ecommerce intent. Building them expands market share, improves AI visibility and supports product discovery — while creating stronger landing pages for future ChatGPT ads. ChatGPT ads shouldn't be a standalone tactic: the site needs to be structured so AI understands what Elite Sports sells, who each product is for, and why it should be recommended.

Next Steps

Elite Sports already has meaningful AI visibility and a strong content base — it just needs SEO cleanup and ecommerce structure. Clean up the technical foundation, strengthen product & category pages, make content AI-ready, and Elite Sports can turn existing authority into stronger product discovery, better market share, more efficient paid media and visibility across both traditional and AI-powered search.
