What Makes This Work

Authentic Stories

Real parents and students sharing their journey. Authentic content outperforms polished ads 3-5x on Meta.

Geo-Targeted + Life Event Audiences

Reach families in the exact communities you serve. Target by zip code, school district, income level, and interests. Meta allows targeting by life events — we can reach recent high school graduates, parents of teens, first-generation families, and multicultural communities actively planning for college.

In-App Lead Forms

Students and parents fill out a form without leaving Facebook/Instagram. Connected to automated email sequence to book appointments.

Google Ad Grants for Nonprofits

Up to $10,000/month in free Google Search Ads

What Is Google Grants?

Google provides eligible nonprofits up to $10,000 per month in free Google Search advertising. On Point for College likely qualifies as a 501(c)(3). This means free ads appearing when parents and students search for college help, financial aid, first-generation college resources, and related terms.

Realistic Grant Utilization — 20% Default

Based on our experience managing Google Grants campaigns, most nonprofits utilize approximately 20% of the $10,000 monthly grant due to bid caps ($2 max CPC), CTR requirements (5%+), and keyword restrictions. We recommend planning around $2,000/month in usable grant budget as a realistic starting point, with optimization to increase utilization over time.

Meta provides the brand awareness and appointment generation through compelling student success stories. Google captures high-intent searches from parents and students actively looking for college support. Together, they cover the full funnel — awareness to action.

Brandastic Recommendations

  1. Phase 1 (Month 1): Discovery, Success Story Strategy & Meta Planning + Google Grants review. Identify 3-5 student success stories for video/photo content. Define target geographies and audiences.
  2. Phase 2 (Months 2-3): Meta Build & Launch + Google Campaign Setup. Set up in-app lead forms and email automation sequence. Begin A/B testing creative (video vs. static, student stories vs. parent stories). Google Grants campaign build.
  3. Phase 3 (Months 3-6+): Meta Optimization & Scaling + Ongoing Google Management. Scale winning creatives, refine audiences, expand to new geographies. Seasonal campaigns (back-to-school, application deadlines, financial aid season), donor acquisition campaigns.
